# SWAP Road Marathon Fueling Plan — Reference Note

**Source:** SWAP Running Patreon ("Road Marathon Fueling Plan!")
**Filed:** April 10, 2026
**Relevance:** Fueling prescription for all plans prescribing marathon
and ultra race-day or long-run nutrition.

---

## Carbohydrate Tiers (g/hr)

Roche prescribes by body weight and experience level:

| Category | Rate | Example athlete |
|----------|------|-----------------|
| Minimum floor | 60 g/hr | First-time marathoner, untrained gut |
| Standard trained | 75-90 g/hr | Experienced road marathoner |
| High-volume trained | 90-120 g/hr | Advanced/ultra, trained gut |
| Peak (Kipchoge-level) | 120+ g/hr | Elite with dedicated GI training |

**"You should never, ever bonk in a marathon."**

GI training is non-negotiable. Practice fueling on every long run at or
above race intensity.

### Carb Sources

- **Multiple transportable carbohydrates** — glucose + fructose blends
  maximize absorption (SGLT1 + GLUT5 transporters)
- Maltodextrin:fructose ratio of ~2:1 most common in commercial products
- Amylopectin clusters (e.g., Maurten, UCAN) for reduced osmolality
- "Whatever you can tolerate" — individual variation is enormous

### Practical Delivery

| Method | Carbs/unit | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Gel (standard) | 20-25g | Most common, easy to carry |
| Gel (double) | 40-50g | Maurten 160, SiS Beta Fuel |
| Drink mix | 30-80g/500ml | Tailored concentration |
| Chews/blocks | 20-25g/packet | Slower absorption, requires chewing |
| Real food (banana) | ~25g | Long runs only, impractical for racing |

To hit 90 g/hr: one double gel every 30 min OR one standard gel every
15 min + drink mix.

## Hydration

| Condition | Rate | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Cool (<15°C) | 400-600 ml/hr | ~16-24 oz/hr |
| Moderate (15-25°C) | 500-800 ml/hr | ~20-32 oz/hr |
| Hot (>25°C) | 600-1000 ml/hr | Max absorption ~1L/hr |

**Sodium:** 500-1000 mg/hr from electrolyte products. Higher for heavy
sweaters, hot conditions.

**"Drink to thirst" is insufficient for the marathon.** Pre-planned
intake is safer. Weigh yourself before and after long runs to calibrate
sweat rate.

## Caffeine

- 3-6 mg/kg bodyweight total, taken in the second half of the race
- "Caffeine is not a substitute for carbs — it's a late-race booster"
- Most gels contain 25-50mg; supplement with caffeine pills if needed
- Habitual caffeine consumers may need 4-6 mg/kg

## Pre-Race Meal

- 3-4 hours before: 2-4g carbs/kg bodyweight
- Low fiber, low fat, moderate protein
- "Your last big meal should be boring — oatmeal, white rice, banana,
  toast with honey"
- Final gel or drink 15-30 min before gun time

## In-Training Fueling Practice

### Long Runs

- Every long run over 90 min: practice race-day fueling protocol
- Start fueling at minute 30-45, not when depleted
- Log what works and what causes GI issues
- By race week: fueling should be automatic, not experimental

### Everyday Runs

- Runs under 60 min: water is sufficient for most athletes
- Runs 60-90 min: optional carb drink
- Fasted runs: "fine occasionally for metabolic flexibility, but not
  as a regular strategy" — running economy degrades when glycogen-depleted

## Ultra-Specific Fueling Extensions

From the Champion Ultra and Beginner Ultra plans:

- 75+ g/hr as the floor for ultras (higher than marathon minimum)
- Real food integration: wraps, rice balls, potatoes alongside gels
- "Practice your aid station routine" — simulate walk-and-eat
- Electrolyte capsules (S-Caps, SaltStick) as supplements
- Anti-nausea: ginger chews, peppermint
- Post-race: 20-40g protein within 30 min, then real meal within 2 hours

## Implications for Generator / Coach

1. All marathon and ultra plans should prescribe fueling practice in
   long-run descriptions: "Practice race fueling: target 75+ g/hr carbs"
2. Fueling reminders belong in structured long runs, not easy days
3. Pre-race briefings should include fueling checklist
4. The coach model should ask about fueling tolerance when reviewing
   long run outcomes
5. Fueling product library (future): should support calculating
   carb/hr rates from product combinations
