---

## Key principles extracted (resource summary)

1. Training can be framed as a **mathematical support system** around race pace.
2. Adjacent speeds (e.g., +/- 5%) act as a ladder of support for race-specific work.
3. Endurance-side support usually carries more weight than speed-side support.
4. General -> race-supportive -> race-specific sequencing is emphasized.
5. Start from **current fitness**, not aspirational goal pace.
6. Workouts should progress by volume, extension, or recovery manipulation.
7. Harder stress should be paired with higher recovery modulation.
8. Pace targets should be updated as capability improves.
9. Same nominal workout pace is not enough; stimulus must evolve over time.
10. A broad speed band is trained, but not all paces are equally race-relevant.
