# Intervals — bedrock narrative (StrideIQ)

**Status:** Reference prose — aligns with `variants/intervals_pilot_v1.md` and calculator policy.  
**Audience:** Product voice, coach copy seeds, onboarding — not a registry schema doc.

---

Intervals are the days with a **spine**: run **hard on purpose for a defined stretch**, take **enough** recovery to do it **right** again, repeat. The work bouts are **short enough to stay honest**—you’re training **toward your ceiling**, not grinding one long “sort of hard” hour. Recovery is **part of the prescription**: it keeps the session **trainable** so the **last** rep still counts.

**Not threshold.** Threshold is **sustained**—one gear you settle into. Intervals are **broken**: work, reset, work. That changes what you stress and how the week has to breathe around the day.

**Not strides on easy.** Strides are a **light neuromuscular touch** after easy running—tiny doses, full recovery, minimal debt when done right. Intervals are a **real quality day** in the weekly budget.

**Paces:** every prescribed interval pace comes from the **Training Pace Calculator**, using the **same recent-race anchor weighting** we use for threshold: within the **last six months**, prefer a qualifying **5K**; if not available, **mile or 10K**, and when more than one race qualifies, prefer the **shorter** performance as the input. From that single anchor, we read the calculator’s **interval / VO2** zone. We show **bands** (same **±5 s/mi** *philosophy* as threshold where numbers appear) because **conditions**—heat, hills, wind, fatigue—change what “on pace” looks like on the ground. Judgment without context burns trust.

**Why it matters:** easy miles, long runs, and marathon-pace work build the **engine**. Threshold teaches **sustained hard**. Intervals train the **ceiling**—turnover, rhythm under real discomfort, and the confidence that hard can still be **controlled**. For 5K/10K goals, intervals are often the **main dish**. For marathoners, they’re often the **sharp tool** used **intelligently** so the week doesn’t become all moderate and marathon pace.

**Intelligently** means: respect the **rest of the week**. If threshold already loaded the system, another **monster** interval session is how people go flat or broken. If nothing touched **speed or rhythm**, a **modest** interval day—or well-placed strides—can **fill the gap** without inventing a second everything-hard day. Training is **touch what needs stimulus, progress when appropriate, recover enough to adapt**.

**Founder thread (illustration, not a guarantee):** intervals were the centerpiece of a **late return** to serious running—**couch to nationally competitive masters** in a short arc. That story is **one N-of-1**; it shows how much **ceiling** work can move when recovery and life allow it. **Threshold** was under-emphasized for a long time; **bringing threshold up** alongside intervals is what’s **opening new plateaus** now. The product lesson: **both** belong in the toolbox; **neither** replaces the other; **the week** has to reconcile them for **this** athlete.

**Advanced shapes**—ladders, mile repeats, long reps like 3×2 mile—are for athletes who’ve **earned** complexity. They’re heavy on the **ledger**; they’re not the default first language.

**Bottom line:** intervals are a **bedrock** because they build **huge engines** when dosed with **calculator-true paces**, **weekly context**, and **honest recovery**—always **N-of-1**, never a generic punishment day.
