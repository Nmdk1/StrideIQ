# SWAP Supplementary Training Plans — Reference Note

**Source:** SWAP Running Patreon plans
**Filed:** April 10, 2026
**Relevance:** Additional plan quality bars for the generator. These plans
cover distances/durations not addressed by the primary plan library.

---

## 1. 6-Week Half Marathon / Threshold Plan

**Duration:** 6 weeks
**Distance:** Half marathon (or threshold improvement without race)
**Volume range:** Intermediate to advanced (same distance-range architecture)
**Dual purpose:** Race prep OR pure threshold development — "the one plan
to get better at everything"

**Key design principles:**
- Shortest race plan in the library. Athletes can pivot off any prior plan.
- "Since output at threshold improves potential at all distances, you can
  do this plan in the winter or before a marathon/ultra build."
- Evaluation option instead of race: 4-6×1mi with 3min recovery to
  measure block gains.

### Wednesday Quality Progression

| Week | Structure | Effort |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | 6-10×2min fast / 90s easy | 5K |
| 2 | 6-10×1min fast / 90s easy + 1-2×10min threshold | 3K + 1hr |
| 3 | 6-12×3min @ threshold (progressive) + 4×30s hills | 1hr + power |
| 4 | 4-8×90s hills + 2-4×5min threshold | 5K + 1hr |
| 5 | 10/8/6/4/2min ladder @ threshold + 4×30s hills | 1hr + power |
| 6 | 3-4×4min @ threshold + 4×45s uphills | 1hr + 3K |

**Progression pattern:** Speed introduction (W1) → speed+threshold
combo (W2) → threshold volume growth (W3-4) → descending ladder (W5)
→ tune-up (W6).

**W3 recovery innovation:** Decreasing recovery within intervals —
1min after first 2 reps, 90s after next 2, 2min after the rest.
Simulates fatigue accumulation within a race.

### Friday Uphill TM Threshold Sessions

| Week | Structure |
|------|-----------|
| 2 | 75-105 min with 4-8×5min @ 1hr effort, 2min recovery |
| 4 | 75-105 min with 3-6×8min @ 1hr effort, 2min recovery |

Extension from 5min → 8min intervals across 2 weeks. Same effort,
longer reps on the TM.

### Saturday Long Run Progression

| Week | Distance | Structured element |
|------|----------|--------------------|
| 1 | 8-16 mi | 3-5×1mi @ 1hr effort, 3min easy recovery |
| 2 | 10-20 mi | Pure easy/mod (aerobic) |
| 3 | 10-20 mi | 20min @ 1hr effort after warm-up |
| 4 | 10-20 mi | Easy (recovery from double quality week) |
| 5 | 10-16 mi | 2-3×2mi @ 1hr effort, 4min easy + 5×45s hills at 3K |
| 6 | 3-6 mi | Shakeout + 4×20s fast + 1min @ HM effort → RACE |

### Implications for Generator

- 6-week plans for HM or as a "threshold improvement block"
- Can be inserted between other plans (base → HM block → race plan)
- Evaluation workout option (repeat W1 Saturday session) for athletes
  not racing
- Uphill TM threshold on Fridays as the second quality session

## 2. 6-Week Track Speed / vVO2 Plan

**Duration:** 6 weeks
**Focus:** Velocity at VO2 max (vVO2) using track workouts
**Volume range:** Distance ranges, 3 plans in 1

**Key design principles:**
- "The only variable that consistently correlates with ultra results
  is vVO2, even at races like UTMB"
- Running economy improvements from this plan transfer to any distance
- Run-on-feel, not splits: "Hitting exact splits is a path to burnout"
- Sodium bicarbonate / Nomio supplementation recommended pre-track
- 75+ g/hr carb fueling on long runs

### Wednesday Track Progression

| Week | Structure | Effort |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | 2-4×(4×400) + 4×200 fast/200 slow | 5K + mile |
| 2 | 2-3×(3×600) + 3-5×300 fast | 10K→5K + 3K-mile |
| 3 | 3-5×(800/400/200) pyramid + 4×200 fast/slow | 10K/5K/3K + mile |
| 4 | OFF TRACK: 4-6×5min threshold + 4-6×30s fast | 1hr + mile |
| 5 | REPEAT W1 (check-in) | 5K + mile |
| 6 | 6-10×800 fast with 400 recovery (evaluation) | 10K→push |

**Key structural elements:**
- W4: deliberate break from track — threshold session instead. "If
  you're extra tired, make it a down week."
- W5 repeats W1 for comparison: "not about going faster, but about
  feeling smoother"
- W6: 800s as final evaluation — "this workout should show you major
  growth over those 6 weeks"
- Float recovery between sets: 200 jog + 400 easy/float (not pure rest)
- Track-specific distances (400, 600, 800, 300, 200)

### Saturday Long Run Progression

| Week | Distance | Structured element |
|------|----------|--------------------|
| 1 | 8-16 mi | Easy/mod + steady progression |
| 2 | 10-16 mi | Easy/mod + steady progression |
| 3 | 10-16 mi | Aerobic fartlek: 1min @ marathon effort every 5min |
| 4 | 12-16 mi | Easy/mod (down week) |
| 5 | 12-16 mi | Easy + 5×30s hills (fatigue resistance) |
| 6 | 10-16 mi | Aerobic fartlek: 1min @ marathon effort, progressing |

No race at the end — just evaluation workouts.

### Sunday Pattern

5-12 mi easy with 5×30s hills near the end. Every Sunday.
"On the hills, aim for a shallow grade of 4-6% and do them around 3k
to mile effort with jog down recovery."

### Implications for Generator

- 6-week vVO2 plan as a standalone "speed block" option
- W4 mid-plan threshold break prevents pure speed burnout
- W5 repeat-W1 as check-in is a powerful evaluation tool
- Track distances (400/600/800) for athletes with track access;
  time-based alternatives for those without
- No race at the end — "how do I know if I'm getting better?"
  answered by the evaluation workout structure

## 3. 16-Week Champion Plan for 100K to 100 Miles

**Duration:** 16 weeks (longest plan in the library)
**Distance:** 100K to 100 miles
**Volume range:** Intermediate to very advanced
**Structure:** Two-peak with embedded mid-cycle race

### Architecture

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Speed + power | 1-3 | Hill intervals, flat VO2, strides |
| Threshold bridge | 4-6 | Uphill TM threshold, steady runs, moderate long runs |
| Aerobic down + stimulus | 6 | Pure aerobic recovery week |
| Race-specific build | 7-9 | Track, longer TM threshold, Power Hour, peak back-to-back |
| **Embedded race/sim** | **10** | **Marathon/50K/50mi race mid-cycle. "Goal: get very sore!"** |
| **Recovery block** | **11** | **Two rest days, minimal running. Absorb the race.** |
| Re-ignition | 12-13 | Track speed + big TM threshold + peak long runs |
| Race-specific | 14 | Power Hour, final big weekend |
| Taper | 15-16 | Reduced volume, sharp stimuli, race |

### Wednesday Quality Progression

| Week | Type | Structure |
|------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Speed (hills) | 6×90s hills + 6×45s hills + 1-2mi moderate |
| 2 | Speed (flat) | 16×1min/1min + 2-3mi steady (50K) |
| 3 | Speed (sets) | 2-3×(4×2min/1min) + 2mi moderate |
| 4 | **Threshold (TM)** | 12-15×3min @ 1hr effort, 90s recovery |
| 5 | Speed (track) | 3-5×(800/400/200) pyramid + 4×200 push |
| 6 | Steady | 10-13mi easy/mod to mod, progressive |
| 7 | Speed (track) | 2-3×(4×600) + 4×200 push |
| 8 | **Threshold (TM)** | 8-12×4min @ 1hr, 90s recovery |
| 9 | Speed (track) | 8-10×800 + 1-2mi steady |
| 10 | Tune-up | 3×5min @ 1hr + 4×45s hills |
| 11 | Rest/recovery | Minimal |
| 12 | Speed (track) | 3-4×(4×400) + 1-2mi moderate |
| 13 | **Threshold (TM)** | 2-4×10min + 2-4×5min @ 1hr |
| 14 | **Threshold (TM)** | 8-12×5min @ 1hr, 1min recovery |
| 15 | Flow | 8-12mi easy/mod over hills |
| 16 | Tune-up | 3×5min @ 1hr + 3×30s hills → RACE |

**TM threshold extension:** W4: 12-15×3min → W8: 8-12×4min → W13:
2-4×10min + 2-4×5min → W14: 8-12×5min with 1min recovery (shrinking).

### Saturday Long Run Progression

| Week | Distance | Structured element |
|------|----------|--------------------|
| 1 | 10-16 mi | Easy/mod, progressing to steady |
| 2 | 13-18 mi | 20min mod/hard @ 1hr effort near start |
| 3 | 16-20 mi | Easy + 1mi harder near end (fatigue resistance) |
| 4 | 12-18 mi | 20min mod/hard + 5min harder near end |
| 5 | 16-20 mi | "Power Hour" — 1hr mod to mod/hard |
| 6 | 10-16 mi | Easy over hills (aerobic down week) |
| 7 | 16-20 mi | 4-7×6min @ 1hr effort, 2min easy |
| 8 | 20-25 mi | Easy with very fast downhills + 5min hard at end |
| 9 | 12-20 mi | Easy (recovery before race week) |
| 10 | BIG RACE | Marathon/50K/50mi mid-cycle |
| 11 | 10-16 mi | Easy (recovery) |
| 12 | 16-20 mi | 20min mod/hard, pushing, with high-carb fueling |
| 13 | 20-25 mi | Easy/mod with fast downhills + 5min hard near end |
| 14 | 14-18 mi | 60-90min mod/hard ("Power Hour" #2) |
| 15 | 10-14 mi | Easy/mod over hills (taper) |
| 16 | 100K/100mi | RACE |

### Unique Elements

**Embedded mid-cycle race (W10):** The defining feature. Marathon, 50K,
or even 50mi race at week 10, followed by a full recovery block (W11).
"Goal: get very sore!" This is supercompensation applied to the macro
level — a massive stimulus followed by major recovery. Can be moved to
weeks 8-10.

**X-train with structured speed (W1-3):** Early x-train days include
intervals: W1 (8×30s fast), W2 (5×(4×1min/1min) sets), W3 (3-4×
3/2/1min sets). X-train becomes purely aerobic from W4+.

**Heat suit training (W11+):** "2×30-60 min with a heat suit each
week within the context of training, keeping heart rate in Z2."
Introduced after the mid-cycle race for hot-race preparation.

**Weight vest hiking doubles:** From W1, for hiking-heavy races.
"20-30 min at 15-20% grade."

**GI training emphasis:** "Really practice GI training, fueling like
the race or more" (W15). 75+ g/hr carb target on all long runs.

**Strength periodization:**
- W1-12: Mountain Legs + Full Strength alternating + squats +
  back extensions
- W13+: Mountain Legs only, no mid-week strength
- W15+: Mountain Legs only, no squats
- Race week: no strength

**Taper:** W15-16 (2 weeks). Option to start earlier if tired at W14.

### Implications for Generator

- 100K-100mi plans require 16 weeks (not 12)
- Two-peak structure with embedded race is unique to this distance
- Generator should offer W10 race options and schedule recovery block
- TM threshold progression spans the full plan with growing interval
  duration and shrinking recovery
- X-train structured speed early → pure aerobic later
- Heat suit and weight vest hiking as race-condition options
