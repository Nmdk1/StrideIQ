# SWAP 12-Week Advanced Marathon Plan — Reference Note

**Source:** David Roche & Megan Roche, MD PhD — SWAP Running Patreon
**Filed:** April 10, 2026
**Relevance:** Plan generator quality bar, marathon workout structures, week-by-week periodization, doubles integration, fueling cues, effort-based prescription, coach voice

---

## Plan Overview

- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Level:** Intermediate to advanced (distance ranges create multiple plans in one)
- **Structure:** 7 days/week with Monday rest, quality Wednesdays, long Saturdays
- **Distinctive features:** Optional doubles on most days, cross-training alternatives,
  effort-based pacing (not pace-based), strength work integrated, heat training,
  hill strides throughout, fueling practice on long runs

## Structural Analysis: How This Differs from Davis

### Effort-Based, Not Pace-Based

The most striking difference: **SWAP never prescribes a pace number.**
Every workout is described by effort:
- "Think 5K effort"
- "Think 10K effort"
- "1-hour effort" (threshold)
- "M effort" (marathon)
- "Relaxed effort" (easy)
- "Purposeful flow" (moderate)

This is fundamentally different from Davis's percentage-based system.
Davis says "105% MP = 4:03/km." SWAP says "think 10K effort."

**Implication for StrideIQ:** The generator should provide BOTH:
a concrete pace number (from RPI) AND an effort descriptor. "Threshold
— 6:45/mi (10K effort)." The pace gives precision. The effort gives
feel. Both together is better than either alone.

### Distance Ranges Instead of Fixed Prescriptions

Every workout has a range: "8-12 mi easy," "14-18 mi easy/mod."
Low end = intermediate. High end = advanced with big base. This makes
one plan serve multiple athletes.

**Implication for plan generator:** The generator should output ranges,
not exact numbers, for easy runs and long runs. The athlete picks
within the range based on how they feel. This aligns with Davis's
"mileage is a range in the specific phase" principle.

### Wednesday Is Quality Day, Always

Every Wednesday is the primary quality session. No exceptions across
12 weeks. This consistency gives athletes a rhythm they can plan
their lives around.

### Saturday Is Long Run Day, Always

Every Saturday is the long run. Saturdays progress from 12-16 mi (W1)
to 20-24 mi (W7) and back down through taper.

### Hill Strides Are Constant Background Stimulus

Nearly every Tuesday and Friday includes short hill strides (4-6×20-30s).
These are NEVER the workout — they're neuromuscular maintenance.
Present in every phase, never progressing significantly in volume.

**Implication for plan generator:** Hill strides should be a background
element on easy days, not a dedicated workout category. They appear
on the calendar as add-ons, not main sessions.

### Optional Doubles Are Everywhere

Doubles appear as options on 3-4 days per week. They're never mandatory.
Options include:
- Uphill treadmill (10-15% grade in Z2 HR for 30-45 min)
- Cross-training (bike, elliptical, 30-60 min)
- Very easy running double (20-30 min)
- Occasionally structured double workout (threshold)

**Implication for plan generator:** Doubles should appear as optional
add-ons on workout days and some easy days. Mark them explicitly as
"Optional." Include cross-training alternatives.

### Strength Is Integrated, Not Separate

Full strength routine appears on Sundays throughout, with deliberate
removal before big weeks and during taper. This is periodized
strength, not random gym work.

### Fueling Cues Are Embedded

Long runs include fueling instructions: "full fueling and hydration,"
"do everything like race day." Race day: "75-90g carbs/hr, 16-24oz
fluid/hr with electrolytes."

**Implication for plan generator:** Workout descriptions should include
fueling cues for long runs and marathon-specific sessions. Not just
paces — the full athlete experience.

## Periodization Comparison

| Week | SWAP Phase | Davis Phase | Key Sessions |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| 1-3 | VO2max introduction + base | General | 16×1min, 8×2min, hill repeats |
| 4-5 | Track introduction + time trial | General → Supportive | 800/400/200 sets, 5K TT, alternating mi at M |
| 6-8 | Big threshold + marathon specific | Supportive | 6-8×5min threshold, 10×800, 3×3mi broken |
| 9-10 | Peak marathon specific | Specific | 5mi tempo predictor, 5/4/3/2/1 at M, final threshold |
| 11-12 | Taper | Specific/Taper | Reduced volume, M-effort touches, race |

SWAP's periodization is smoother than Davis's — less rigid phase
boundaries, more gradual intensity shift. VO2max work (the short fast
repeats) appears throughout, not just in the general phase. Marathon
pace work appears as early as week 1 (1mi at M effort at the end of
the interval session).

This aligns with Canova's "add, not replace" principle — SWAP never
drops a workout type entirely, just shifts the emphasis.

## Workout Progressions (Extension-Based)

### 800m Track Repeats (Speed Support)

- Week 4: 4-5×(800/400/200) mixed set — introduction
- Week 7: 10×800 with 400 easy — established
- Week 10: 10×800 with 400 easy — revisited for comparison

"Revisiting this workout one more time, hopefully with some progress!
It's ok if not though — we aren't training for 800 workouts."

This IS extension-based progression and build-over-build comparison,
but with Roche's characteristic psychological grace.

### Threshold (1-Hour Effort)

- Week 2: 6×5min at 1-hour effort, 2min recovery
- Week 6: 6-8×5min at 1-hour effort, 90s recovery (shorter rest)
- Week 10: 4×10min at 1-hour effort, 3min recovery (longer segments)

Extension: 5min → 5min (shorter rest) → 10min segments. Same effort.

### Marathon Pace Specific

- Week 4: 10-12mi alternating 1mi at 1-hour effort / 1mi float
- Week 6: 3×3mi (1mi 10K effort → 2mi M effort), 1mi recovery
- Week 7: 5/4/3/2/1 mi at M effort, 1mi float — PEAK (20-24mi total)
- Week 9: 4-5×3mi (2mi M + 1mi faster), 1mi float
- Week 11: 2×2mi at M effort, 1mi float (taper touch)

This is the descending-set marathon specific workout: 5/4/3/2/1 miles
at M effort with float recovery. It's Roche's version of the
alternating-km session — same principle, effort-based instead of
pace-based, with descending sets that get psychologically easier as
fatigue accumulates.

## Quality Bar: What SWAP Workout Descriptions Sound Like

These are the target for the plan generator's description quality:

**Good coach notes in the plan:**
- "The vVO2 intervals into M-effort work will be taxing at first!"
- "The workout will feel extra tough with muscular fatigue and lactate accumulation!"
- "This steady run will have direct translation to marathon ability later"
- "Think smooth and strong, so that you can transition into float recoveries"
- "Do everything like race day"
- "Go on the low end of the range if you feel tired at all!"
- "Revisiting this workout one more time, hopefully with some progress! It's ok if not though"
- "BELIEVE. You got this!"

The voice: encouraging, honest about difficulty, connecting the workout
to the goal, giving permission to back off. Never robotic. Never
punitive. Never vague.

## What NOT to Do

- Do NOT prescribe workouts without effort context. "8×800m at 3:12"
  is less useful than "8×800m at 10K effort (~3:12/800m)."
- Do NOT make doubles mandatory. Every SWAP double says "Optional."
- Do NOT skip fueling cues on long runs. Athletes need to practice
  race-day nutrition during training.
- Do NOT drop workout types entirely during a phase. VO2max touches
  (short fast strides, 200m reps) appear throughout the SWAP plan,
  even during marathon-specific weeks.
- Do NOT schedule two hard days back-to-back. SWAP puts quality on
  Wednesday and long run on Saturday — 2 hard days separated by 2
  easy days.
- Do NOT make the taper too aggressive. SWAP's taper (weeks 11-12)
  still includes quality work — just shorter and at the low end of
  ranges. The sharpening sessions keep the athlete's legs responsive.
