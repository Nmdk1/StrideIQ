## Part 1: The Origin Story

### The Problem We Couldn't Solve With Existing Tools

Every serious runner knows the frustration: you have years of data locked in Strava, Garmin, or Coros. You run consistently. You log everything. But when you ask the obvious question—**"Am I actually getting better?"**—you get silence.

The data exists. The insights don't.

Strava shows you maps. Garmin shows you VO2max estimates that fluctuate randomly. Training Peaks gives you TSS numbers without context. Apps give you badges and kudos. None of them answer the question that matters: **Is this working?**

### The Founder's Perspective

Michael Shaffer, 57, has been running for decades. He's chased PRs into his late 50s, dealt with injuries, navigated the complex question of how to train as an aging athlete without buying into the "decline narrative" that dominates the industry.

He wanted a system that:
- Treated him as an individual, not a demographic
- Learned from HIS data, not global averages
- Told him the truth about what's working and what isn't
- Applied the knowledge of elite coaches without the $200/month price tag
- Never assumed he should "slow down" because of age

That system didn't exist. So he built it.

## Part 2: What StrideIQ Actually Is

### The Core Philosophy: N=1 Intelligence

StrideIQ is built on a radical premise: **your body is the only dataset that matters.**

We don't care what "runners your age" typically do. We don't apply population averages. We don't make assumptions based on demographics.

We build **personal response curves** from YOUR data only:
- How YOUR body responds to training load
- What sleep quality does to YOUR next-day performance
- Which workout types produce adaptation for YOU
- How long YOUR recovery actually takes

The longer you use StrideIQ, the smarter it gets about YOU specifically.

### The Three Pillars

**1. Performance Efficiency (The Master Signal)**

Everything we measure ties back to one question: Are you producing more speed for the same physiological cost?

- Pace @ Heart Rate
- Heart Rate @ Pace  
- Age-graded performance %

If you're running faster at the same heart rate—or the same pace at a lower heart rate—you're improving. Period. Everything else is noise.

**2. Personal Correlation Engine**

We discover what actually moves the needle for YOU:
- Sleep → next-day efficiency correlation
- Nutrition → long-run fade patterns
- Training load → adaptation timing
- Recovery patterns → injury risk signals

No guessing. No "best practices." Just what YOUR data shows.

**3. The Athlete Intelligence Bank**

Every workout, every race, every build—we learn and bank the insights:
- What workouts produce adaptation for YOU
- What preceded your injuries
- What your recovery patterns look like
- What doesn't work (intervals mid-build → breakdown)

Year one is learning. Year two is precision. Year three+, we know you better than you know yourself.

## Part 3: The Technology

### AI-Powered Coaching (Not AI-Generated Plans)

The AI in StrideIQ isn't a plan generator. It's a reasoning engine that:
- Synthesizes knowledge from Daniels, Pfitzinger, Canova, Hudson, Roche, Bitter, and more
- Combines that knowledge with YOUR diagnostic signals
- Provides context-aware answers that cite YOUR data
- Never hallucinates—every claim is grounded in evidence

We use multiple models strategically:
- GPT-4o-mini for most interactions (fast, capable)
- Claude Opus for high-stakes decisions (injury, major changes)
- All answers cite receipts—the actual data behind the recommendation

### VDOT-Based Training Paces

We don't guess your training paces. We calculate them from your actual race performances using Jack Daniels' VDOT methodology:
- Easy pace
- Marathon pace  
- Threshold pace
- Interval pace
- Repetition pace

One PB from a standard distance (5K, 10K, half marathon, marathon) gives us everything we need.

### Age-Graded Performance (WMA Standards)

We use official World Masters Athletics age-grading to normalize performance across ages. A 60-year-old running 3:30 for a marathon is performing at a higher level than a 30-year-old running 3:15.

This isn't about making excuses. It's about measuring real performance fairly.
