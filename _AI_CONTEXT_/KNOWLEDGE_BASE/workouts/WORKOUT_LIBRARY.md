## Detailed Workout Definitions

### Recovery Runs
- **Duration:** 20-45 min
- **Intensity:** Very easy, below 75% MHR
- **Pace:** Conversational, 1:30-2:00+ slower than race pace
- **Purpose:** Active recovery, blood flow without stress
- **When:** Day after hard workout or race

### Easy Runs
- **Duration:** 30-90 min
- **Intensity:** Easy, 72-81% MHR
- **Pace:** Conversational, can talk in full sentences
- **Purpose:** Aerobic base building, recovery
- **When:** Majority of weekly mileage

### Long Runs (Easy)
- **Duration:** 1-3+ hours
- **Intensity:** Easy to moderate, 75-83% MHR
- **Pace:** 10-20% slower than marathon pace
- **Purpose:** Endurance, fat oxidation, mental toughness
- **When:** Weekly, typically weekend

### Medium-Long Runs
- **Duration:** 70-75% of long run distance
- **Intensity:** Same as long run
- **Purpose:** Build endurance without full recovery cost
- **When:** Mid-week endurance builder
- **Example:** If LR = 20 mi, MLR = 14-15 mi

### Tempo Runs
- **Duration:** 10-40 min at tempo
- **Intensity:** "Comfortably hard," 83-90% MHR
- **Pace:** ~10-15 sec/mi slower than 10K pace
- **Purpose:** Improve lactate threshold
- **When:** 1-2x per week during build phase

### Tempo Intervals
- **Duration:** 5-20 min per interval
- **Recovery:** 1-2 min jog
- **Intensity:** Same as tempo
- **Purpose:** Threshold work with recovery
- **When:** Alternative to continuous tempo

### Cruise Intervals
- **Duration:** 3-8 min per interval
- **Recovery:** 30-60 sec jog
- **Intensity:** T pace
- **Purpose:** Threshold with rhythm focus
- **When:** Build to continuous tempo

### VO2max Intervals
- **Duration:** 3-5 min per rep
- **Recovery:** Equal to or slightly less than work time
- **Intensity:** 5K race pace, 93-95% MHR
- **Purpose:** Increase aerobic power
- **Limit:** Max 8% of weekly mileage OR 10K

### Repetitions (R)
- **Duration:** 200m-600m (under 2 min)
- **Recovery:** 2-3x work duration
- **Intensity:** Faster than 5K pace
- **Purpose:** Speed, economy, anaerobic power
- **Limit:** Max 5% of weekly mileage OR 5 miles

### Strides
- **Distance:** 80-120m
- **Intensity:** Fast but relaxed (not sprinting)
- **Recovery:** Full (walk back)
- **Purpose:** Running economy, neuromuscular activation
- **When:** After easy runs, 4-6 reps

### Hill Sprints
- **Duration:** 8-10 seconds
- **Intensity:** Maximum effort
- **Recovery:** Full (2-3 min)
- **Grade:** 6-10% incline
- **Purpose:** Neuromuscular power, economy
- **When:** After easy runs, 6-12 reps

### Hill Repetitions
- **Duration:** 2-5 min uphill
- **Intensity:** 5K effort
- **Recovery:** Jog down
- **Purpose:** Leg strength, power endurance
- **When:** Base/build phase

### Fartlek Runs
- **Duration:** Variable (30 sec - 5 min bursts)
- **Intensity:** 5K-10K effort for fast portions
- **Recovery:** Easy jog between
- **Purpose:** Speed + endurance, flexible
- **Examples:**
  - 8 mi easy with 4 × 3 min @ 10K
  - 6 mi with 10 × 1 min @ 5K effort
  - "Feel-based" pickups throughout

### Progression Runs
- **Duration:** Any length
- **Structure:** Start slow, finish fast
- **Variants:**
  - **Thirds:** 1/3 slow, 1/3 steady, 1/3 fast
  - **DUSA:** 75-90% easy, then faster
  - **Super Fast Finish:** Easy + sprint last few min
- **Purpose:** Teaches pacing, builds finishing strength

### Fast Finish Long Run (FFLR)
- **Duration:** Long run distance
- **Structure:** Easy pace → faster final 20-30%
- **Final pace:** Near race effort
- **Purpose:** Simulate race fatigue, mental toughness
- **Example:** 18 mi: 14 mi easy, 4 mi @ MP

### Marathon Pace Runs
- **Duration:** 8-16 miles at MP
- **Intensity:** Goal marathon pace, 81-88% MHR
- **Purpose:** Race-specific fitness, confidence
- **When:** Build and peak phase

### Specific-Endurance Long
- **Duration:** Long run with intervals
- **Structure:** Easy + race pace segments + easy
- **Example:** 1 hr easy, 15 × 1 min @ MP / 1 min easy, 30 min easy
- **Purpose:** Race simulation with controlled fatigue

### Goal Pace Run
- **Duration:** Variable
- **Intensity:** Target race pace exactly
- **Purpose:** Pace familiarity, confidence
- **When:** Race-specific phase

## Auto-Classification Logic

### Primary Indicators
1. **Average pace** vs. known race paces
2. **Average HR** vs. zones
3. **Duration** of effort
4. **Pace variability** (steady vs. intervals)
5. **Elevation profile** (hills detection)

### Classification Hierarchy
1. If HR < 75% MHR for entire run → **Recovery**
2. If HR 72-83% and steady pace → **Easy/Long**
3. If intervals detected at 5K pace → **VO2max Intervals**
4. If intervals detected at threshold → **Tempo Intervals**
5. If progressive (each segment faster) → **Progression Run**
6. If steady at marathon pace → **MP Run**
7. If short bursts (<2 min) at max effort → **Repetitions/Strides**
8. If uphill bursts → **Hill Work**
